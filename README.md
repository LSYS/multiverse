We investigate the dependence of GPS-based home location detection on a multiude of analyst choices.
Using finegrained GPS traces in Singapore, we sweep xxx such choices, producing yyy distinct home assignment configurations across xxx window and yyy devices.
In the absence of individual-level ground truth, we consider three validation metrics:
(i) residential zone overlap, 
(ii) census resident registers, and 
(iii) dwelling unit density.
We propagate each configuration into a downstream application(??) that relies on home assignment and document how these resulting quantities shift.

## 📚 Home Detection Algorithm (HDA) Literature

## 🛰️ GPS strand

GPS data comes from smartphone location-based services (LBS), providing lat/lon coordinates at high frequency.
Spatial resolution is typically meters (with horizontal accuracy filtering, often ≤50m).
Observations are passively and near-continuously collected, so temporal coverage is dense but variable across devices.

6. **Verma, Mittal, Lei, Chen & Ukkusuri (2024).** Comparison of home detection algorithms using smartphone GPS data. *EPJ Data Science* 13, 6.
   GPS, 4 US cities, 8 datasets, 5 algorithms, 3 proxy validation metrics, 2 downstream case studies.
   [Link](https://doi.org/10.1140/epjds/s13688-023-00447-w)

7. **Recalde et al. (2025).** Design and validation of a grid-based home detection via stay-time (GHOST) software for mobile location data. *arXiv* 2605.20429.
   GPS, square grid, sweeps grid size and temporal parameters, Boston + 10 volunteers with known addresses.
   [Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20429)

### 📞 CDR strand

CDR (Call Detail Records) logs are generated by telecom operators when a phone makes a call or sends an SMS.
Location is recorded at the cell-tower level (Voronoi polygons), not as a lat/lon coordinate.
Spatial resolution is hundreds of meters to kilometers depending on tower density.
Observations are event-triggered (only when the user actively uses the phone), so temporal coverage is sparse and irregular.

1. **Vanhoof, Reis, Ploetz & Smoreda (2018).** Assessing the quality of home detection from mobile phone data for official statistics. *Journal of Official Statistics* 34(4), 935--960.
   CDR, France, 18M users, 225 setups (9 algorithms × time periods × parameters). Census validation at Voronoi level.
   [Link](https://doi.org/10.2478/jos-2018-0046)

2. **Vanhoof, Lee & Smoreda (2020).** Performance and sensitivities of home detection on mobile phone data. In *Big Data Meets Survey Science*, Wiley, 245--271.
   Expanded version of the above.
   [Link](https://doi.org/10.1002/9781118976357.ch8)

3. **Pappalardo, Ferres, Sacasa, Cattuto & Bravo (2021).** Evaluation of home detection algorithms on mobile phone data using individual-level ground truth. *EPJ Data Science* 10(1), 29.
   CDR/XDR/CPR, Chile, 65 users with known addresses.
   [Link](https://doi.org/10.1140/epjds/s13688-021-00284-9)

4. **Oosterlinck, Baecke & Benoit (2021).** Home location prediction with telecom data: Benchmarking heuristics with a predictive modelling approach. *Expert Systems with Applications* 170, 114507.
   CDR, Belgian telecom, individual-level ground truth. Supervised ML outperforms heuristics.
   [Link](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eswa.2020.114507)
